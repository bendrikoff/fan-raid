# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Football fans using a Telegram Mini App or mobile browser as a second screen while a live match is running. They are in a fast, distracted context: watching the match, checking the score, choosing a side, and answering short predictions before the answer window closes.

## Product Purpose

Fan Raid turns a football match into a shared live raid. Players choose Brazil or Germany, answer micro-predictions during play, earn points, and shift Fan Power for their side. Success means the user can understand the match state, the active question, their own score, and the raid momentum at a glance.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, live, competitive. The interface should feel like a broadcast control panel for fans rather than a betting app, casino surface, or generic game menu.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like a betting slip, fantasy-casino lobby, purple SaaS dashboard, or generic mobile game with oversized emoji and gradient cards. Avoid decorative motion that competes with the live question.

## Design Principles

- Live state first: score, minute, Fan Power, and question priority must be immediately clear.
- Server authority should feel trustworthy: accepted, locked, resolved, and missed states need precise visual feedback.
- Team energy, not visual noise: use team colors as functional accents, not as full-screen decoration.
- Mobile under pressure: controls must be large enough to hit quickly and stable enough not to shift during ticks.
- Broadcast restraint: make the UI feel active through hierarchy, timing, and state, not through decorative clutter.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Do not rely on color alone for correct/wrong/team states. Respect reduced-motion preferences for pulses, shakes, flashes, and entrance animation.
